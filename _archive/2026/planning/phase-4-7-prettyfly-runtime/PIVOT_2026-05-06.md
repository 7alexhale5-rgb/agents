# Pivot

## §1 What changed and why

Operator decision tonight: skip the 7-night G1 Hermes baseline measurement. Build PrettyFly Runtime directly from a synthesis of Hermes + Gravity Claw + Paperclip + OpenClaw + agency-agents.

Justification (verbatim): _"we don't need it to work well if we're going to rip apart the apartment and build a new one anyway, so we can dictate exactly how we want it to be once we have the new living quarters established."_

This aligns with Jack Roberts' bare-metal thesis (the original instinct behind Gravity Claw): own the loop, cherry-pick the patterns, treat third-party frameworks as reference architecture, not runtime. Gravity Claw was Alex's first attempt at this thesis; PF Runtime is the v2 with multi-profile + production pipes added.

The 7-night G1 wait was the only thing blocking sub-phase 4.7.1. With G1 skipped, 4.7.1 build starts NOW.

## §6 Risks of skipping the baseline

1. **Quality floors are architectural beliefs, not empirical**. The 0.80 Wilson and 0.83 Ragas thresholds are reasonable but unvalidated. Mitigation: the 48h micro-pilot in sub-phase E is now doubly important — it becomes the first empirical observation, and Gate 4's latency threshold is calibrated as `observed-p95 + 30% headroom` rather than a fixed number.

2. **Latency gate is approximate**. 2000ms wall-clock is a reasonable Slack-interaction floor but not derived from observation. Mitigation: 2-hour load test under realistic tool-call mix (not synthetic prompts) before the 14-day shadow clock starts. ConsultOps lead-routing with multiple LAIK MCP round-trips is the worst case to instrument.

3. **No directional cost signal**. Without G1, we don't know what Hermes was costing per session, so we can't estimate the cutover savings. Mitigation: LiteLLM `/spend/logs` already tracks per-key spend; that becomes the cost telemetry post-cutover regardless of what Hermes was doing.
